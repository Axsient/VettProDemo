VettPro Executive Dashboard: Strategic Command Center

The executive dashboard is designed to answer three fundamental questions for a C-suite user:

    "What is our overall risk exposure right now?"

    "Where are we losing money or creating efficiencies?"

    "What emerging threats do I need to be aware of?"

Tier 1: Critical, At-a-Glance KPIs

This top section of the dashboard should be a series of clean, prominent KPI cards that provide an instant snapshot of the business's health from a risk perspective.
Critical KPIs & Information:

    Overall Supplier Risk Exposure (Donut Chart & Score):

        What it is: A large, color-coded donut chart showing the percentage of all active suppliers currently classified as Low, Medium, High, or Critical risk.

        Why it's critical: This is the single most important metric. It tells an executive the overall risk profile of the entire supply chain in one look. A rising percentage of high-risk suppliers is an immediate call to action.

        Data Source: Aggregated riskLevel from the supplierSample data.

    Fraud & Financial Loss Prevention (KPI Card):

        What it is: A single, large number showing the total ZAR value of potentially fraudulent invoices flagged by the "Invoice DNA" engine in the last quarter.

        Why it's critical: This directly quantifies the ROI of the platform. It's a hard financial metric that demonstrates how VettPro is protecting the bottom line.

        Data Source: Sum of total_amount from invoices where fraud_score is above a high threshold (e.g., > 0.8).

    Operational Efficiency: Time-to-Onboard (KPI Card):

        What it is: The average number of days it takes to move a new, critical supplier from "Initiated" to "Completed" and fully onboarded.

        Why it's critical: This measures the speed and efficiency of the procurement and risk management process. A long onboarding time can delay critical projects and indicates operational bottlenecks.

        Data Source: Average of actualCompletionDate - dateInitiated for VettingCase where priority is 'High'.

    Compliance Status: Overdue Post-Vetting (KPI Card):

        What it is: A count of high-risk suppliers whose scheduled post-vetting checks are currently overdue.

        Why it's critical: This highlights immediate compliance gaps. These are suppliers who were previously approved but whose risk status has not been re-validated, creating a significant blind spot.

        Data Source: Count of scheduled_jobs where status is 'Scheduled' and scheduled_for_date is in the past.

Tier 2: AI-Powered Strategic Insights

This section leverages the AI capabilities of the platform to provide forward-looking and deep insights that a standard report cannot.
Critical AI Insights:

    Emerging Risk Hotspots (AI-Generated List):

        What it is: A dynamic list, generated by the AI engine, of the top 3-5 systemic risks identified across the entire supplier base. This is not just a list of risky suppliers, but of risk themes.

        Examples:

            "Increased Financial Distress: 15 suppliers in the Logistics category are showing signs of financial instability (late payments, poor credit)."

            "Director Interlock: A single individual has been identified as a director on 7 different supplier companies, 3 of which are in a high-risk category."

            "Geographic Concentration: 45% of all Critical risk suppliers are operating out of a single province (e.g., KwaZulu-Natal), indicating a potential supply chain vulnerability."

        Why it's critical: This is the predictive power of VettPro. It moves beyond individual supplier risk to show systemic, strategic threats to the business that require executive-level attention.

Good-to-Have AI Insights:

    Invoice DNA Anomaly of the Month (AI-Highlighted Example):

        What it is: A small card showcasing the single most interesting or egregious finding from the invoice analysis engine. For example: "Flagged: An invoice from 'ABC Supplies' for 100 hard hats at R5,000 each, when the agreed RFP price was R500 each."

        Why it's good to have: It provides a concrete, easily understandable example of the fraud detection system in action, making the value proposition very real.

Tier 3: Good-to-Have Overviews

This section provides broader context and allows for high-level exploration without getting into operational weeds.
Good-to-Have Visualizations:

    Geographic Risk Concentration (Interactive Map):

        What it is: A map of South Africa with provinces color-coded based on the concentration of high-risk suppliers. An executive could hover over Gauteng and see a summary tooltip.

        Why it's good to have: It provides a powerful visual tool for understanding geographic dependencies and supply chain vulnerabilities.

        Data Source: supplierSample data aggregated by province.

    Risk Trend Over Time (Line Chart):

        What it is: A simple line chart showing the trend of the "Overall Supplier Risk Exposure" score over the last 12-24 months.

        Why it's good to have: It answers the question, "Is our overall risk profile getting better or worse over time?" It provides a historical perspective on the effectiveness of risk management strategies.

    Vetting Project Portfolio Snapshot (Simple Table):

        What it is: A highly condensed table showing only major, strategic vetting projects (from the "Project-Based Vetting" feature).

        Columns: Project Name, % Complete, Overall Risk Finding (e.g., "15% of suppliers flagged").

        Why it's good to have: It gives executives visibility into proactive, strategic risk mitigation initiatives that are underway, beyond the day-to-day reactive vetting.

This structure ensures that an executive can open the dashboard, understand the company's risk posture in under 30 seconds from the critical KPIs, and then choose to explore the deeper strategic insights offered by the AI and trend visualizations.
